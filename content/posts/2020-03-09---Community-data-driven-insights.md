In 2018, UM and MUMC+ launched their first and joint ‘data-driven’ initiative towards FAIR university. The Community for Data Driven Insights (CDDI) governance structure is in place and they explore the ways to turn its FAIR ambitions into reality. CDDI is designed to open up a space where relevant stakeholders work together on specific issues for the realisation of the 2030 Agenda.

This community group have diverse backgrounds, but tend to converge as to their shared vision of becoming a FAIR University in 2023. They are an interdisciplinary community of researchers and service providers that has arised organically to help facilitate the change toward improved research data reuse and sharing. The mission of CDDI is to ameliorate research practices by supporting researchers in the ways knowledge is created and shared across research disciplines and communities. They look at how CDDI can effectively interlink researchers, data experts, services and training needs, projects and organizations. Likewise, they exchange lessons learnt and discuss potential action plans to make responsible research by implementing FAIR.
